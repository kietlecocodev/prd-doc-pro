# Good Example: Problem Statement (Mobile Fintech)

**Score: 10/10** — Quantified, user-centered, business-connected

---

## Problem Statement

### The Pain
62% of our mobile banking users who initiate a peer-to-peer transfer abandon the flow before completion. Exit-screen analysis (N=1,200 sessions, Jan-Mar 2026) shows three failure points:

1. **Recipient lookup friction** (38% of abandons): Users must type the full phone number or email. No contacts integration, no recent recipients, no QR code option.
2. **Confirmation anxiety** (31% of abandons): The confirmation screen shows only the recipient's phone number — no name, no photo, no verification. Users fear sending money to the wrong person.
3. **Speed perception** (21% of abandons): The "Processing..." screen shows a spinner with no progress indication. Average transfer time is 4.2 seconds, but perceived wait time (user research, N=50) is "over 15 seconds."

### Business Impact
- **$2.3M/month** in unrealized transaction volume (based on average abandoned transfer of $127 × 18,200 abandoned transfers/month)
- **340 support tickets/month** tagged "transfer-failed" — 78% are actually successful transfers where users thought it failed and re-sent
- **App store rating**: 23 of last 100 one-star reviews mention "can't send money" or "transfer stuck"
- **Competitor benchmark**: Cash App completes P2P in 2 taps with contact photo confirmation. Our flow requires 5 taps with no visual confirmation.

### Who Is Affected
- **Primary**: Casual senders (72% of P2P users) — send 1-3 transfers/month, low tolerance for friction
- **Secondary**: Power senders (28%) — send 10+ transfers/month, compensate with workarounds (copy-paste numbers) but express frustration in NPS comments (NPS for this segment: 12 vs. overall 34)

## Why This Is Good

| Criterion | How It's Met |
|-----------|-------------|
| Quantified pain | 62% abandon rate, $2.3M/mo, 340 tickets, 23 reviews |
| No solution leaking | Describes what users suffer, never mentions what to build |
| Specific personas | Casual senders vs power senders with distinct behaviors |
| Business impact | Revenue, support cost, app store rating, competitor gap |
| Evidence-backed | N=1,200 sessions, N=50 user research, support ticket tags |
| Actionable | Three clear failure points that can each be addressed |
